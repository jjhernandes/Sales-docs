"Sales reps win or lose their calls in the first 2 minutes, and a lot of the time, they don't even realize it. And by the time that they do. It's already too late. Most closers spend their whole career trying to fix this with better techniques. But the technique was never the problem.

Most closers have already lost by minute two, not because of anything they said, but because of everything that happened before they said it. Because the first ten minutes are not the warm up. They are the sale.

Right now, you might be treating the opening like small talk. 'Hey, how are you doing? Where are you calling from? How's the weather?' Because you think you need to build likability before respect. You think a little small talk will build that. But here's the thing: the prospect has already decided whether they respect you or not before you even ask your first real question.

Because the first ten minutes are not about rapport. They're about building a mutual level of respect, transparency, and authority with the prospect. Without respect and authority, objection handling is pointless, and the prospect won't trust you enough to invest in the program you're selling.

After working with some of the biggest sales teams in the industry. Alex Hormozi, Iman Gadzhi, Ashton Hall, Daniel Iles. And running Sales.io, where we've trained over 2,000+ sales reps. I've noticed that the pattern never changes. The closers who win don't 'close' better. But they follow this framework perfectly.

I call it The First 120. It's responsible for millions of dollars in cash collected, made up of four key things that let you build that level of respect and authority at the beginning of the call, rather than lose it at the end. So let's dive in.

Number one. Respect before likability. Most closers optimize for being liked. But the best closers optimize for being respected. These are two completely different things, and the prospect responds to them differently. Build respect first, and the likability will follow.

Number two. Opening with a strong agenda. This is where the actual leadership starts. Most closers never do this. But this is how I actually set the agenda...

Number three. The first two minutes. The sale is always won or lost if trust isn't created here. Rapport looks like... And trust, it looks like... The prospect is deciding whether or not to listen to you and trust you in the first two minutes. Whether you close or not is directly downstream of what happens here.

Number four. Authority and respect as a prerequisite. Objection handling only works if your presence as an authority is already there. Because without it, they won't trust you enough to let you lead them into making a big decision. Especially if they're type A. I've seen this inside Hormozi's operation. Inside Iman's operation. The closers who never established authority never recovered from it. So here's what authority actually sounds like on a call...

So here's the one thing to change on your very next call. Open the agenda. Say what you're there to do, and why. Lead with respect before likability, from the first second. The prospect is deciding in the first two minutes. Be worth deciding for.

Most closers lose their calls in the first 2 minutes. And now you know what to fix.

If you want to work on this at a higher level... Sales.io is in the description. And if you want more of this, I'm sharing these things weekly on my channel.

I'll see you in the next one."
