Full Script: Cold Open to Sign Off

Every line, in order, written to be read straight through and delivered to camera. Bracketed cues are direction, not dialogue, skip them when reading out loud. The bullet-point production notes further down in this doc are the same material broken out by section for planning and filming reference.

Cold Open

"Sales reps win or lose their calls in the first 2 minutes, and a lot of the time, they don't even realize it. And by the time that they do. It's already too late. Most closers spend their whole career trying to fix this with better techniques. But the technique was never the problem.

Most closers have already lost by minute two. They just don't know it yet. It's not because of anything they said. It's because of everything that happened before they said it.

The first ten minutes aren't the warm up. They're the sale."

The Problem

"Here's what's actually happening on your calls right now. You're treating the opening like small talk. You're waiting for the 'real' conversation to start. You're going for likability before respect, and that's backwards.

The prospect has already decided something about you before you've even asked your first real question."

The Reframe

"So let's reframe this. The first ten minutes aren't about rapport. They're about authority.

Authority is the prerequisite. Everything else builds on top of it. Without it, objection handling is pointless, because the prospect isn't taking your word for anything.

After working with some of the biggest sales teams in the industry. Alex Hormozi, Iman Gadzhi, Ashton Hall, Daniel Iles.

And running Sales.io, where we've trained over 2,000+ sales reps. I've noticed that the pattern never changes. The closers who win don't 'close' better. But they follow this framework perfectly."

The Framework

"I call it The First 120. Four things that dictate whether or not a call closes, in the first few minutes. Let's dive in.

Number one. Respect before likability.
Most closers are optimizing to be liked. The best closers are optimizing to be respected. Those aren't the same thing, and the prospect can feel the difference immediately. Respect first. Likability follows. demonstrate the first 60 seconds

Number two. Opening the agenda.
This is where leadership starts. Almost none of them do this. Genuinely, almost none. demonstrate the exact opening line The prospect relaxes the moment someone takes control of the room. You can't lead a call you never opened.

Number three. The first two minutes.
If trust never gets built here, the sale is already lost. Not rapport. Trust. show the difference on a real call The prospect is deciding right now whether you're worth listening to. Everything after this is downstream of what happens here.

Number four. Authority as a prerequisite.
Objection handling only works if the authority is already there. Without it, the prospect isn't taking your word for anything. You can't install authority mid call. It has to be there from the start. show what authority actually sounds like I've seen this inside Hormozi's operation. Inside Iman's operation. The closers who never established authority never recovered from it."

The Takeaway

"So here's the one thing to change on your very next call. Open the agenda. Say what you're there to do, and why. Lead with respect before likability, from the first second. The prospect is deciding in the first two minutes. Be worth deciding for."

Outro

"Most closers lose their calls in the first 2 minutes. And now you know what to fix.

If you want to work on this at a higher level... Sales.io is in the description. And if you want more of this, I'm sharing these things weekly on my channel.

I'll see you in the next one."
