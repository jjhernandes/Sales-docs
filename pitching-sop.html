Sales Operations

Pitching SOP

How to structure and deliver a pitch that earns a decision — not just a "let me think about it." This applies to any sales call once discovery is complete.

🎯
What a Pitch Actually Is

A pitch is not a presentation. It is a targeted response to what you just learned in discovery. If your pitch sounds the same for every prospect, you did not listen well enough.

The pitch only works after you have built enough understanding of the prospect's situation to make it relevant to them specifically. Lead with it and you are just another rep talking at them.

Your goal in the pitch is not to impress — it is to connect what you offer to the specific pain they described. If they cannot see themselves in what you are saying, it will not land.

📐
Pre-Pitch Checklist

You have completed discovery and understand their current situation, their pain, and what they are trying to accomplish.

You know who is in the room and whether they are the decision-maker or an influencer.

You have a sense of their budget comfort or at least their investment expectations.

You have identified the one or two things that matter most to them — this is what you lead your pitch with.

Your demo, case study, or supporting material is ready to pull up without fumbling.

🗂️
The Pitch Structure
01

Reflect Back What You Heard

Before introducing your solution, summarize what they told you. This shows you listened, builds trust, and puts the problem front of mind before you offer the answer. Keep it to 2-3 sentences.

02

Bridge to Your Solution

Connect what they said to what you do. Not a generic description of your offer — a targeted connection. "Based on what you described, here is what I think makes sense for you specifically..."

03

Explain How It Works

Walk through the mechanism simply and clearly. How does your solution actually solve the problem? Avoid jargon. If you cannot explain it in plain language, they cannot buy it with confidence.

04

Show Proof

A real example, a case study, or a live demonstration. Telling people it works is weak. Showing them is what builds conviction. Match the proof to their situation as closely as possible.

05

Name What Makes You Different

One or two things — not ten. What is the reason they should choose you over the alternatives? Be specific. "We are the best" is not a differentiator. A specific, verifiable edge is.

06

Check for Understanding

Stop after each key point and ask: "Does this make sense so far?" or "Does this connect to what you described?" Do not power through the whole pitch and then ask if they have questions. Check in throughout.

07

Anchor on Value Before Price

Before you name the investment, get a verbal yes to the concept. "Based on everything we have covered — does this look like the kind of thing that would move the needle for you?" If they say yes, the price conversation is a logistics conversation, not a sales battle.

08

Present the Investment

State the price clearly and without apology. Pause after you say it. Do not fill the silence. The silence is doing work — let it. How they respond tells you exactly where to go next.

⚡
Pitch Delivery Standards

Conversational, not presentational. You are talking with someone, not at them. Every 2-3 minutes, pull them into the conversation with a question.

Pace down. When reps are nervous or rushing, they talk faster. Slow down. Confidence lives in the pause.

Use their words. When you reflect back their pain, use the exact language they used. Not a synonym — their words. It signals you heard them precisely.

Do not oversell. When a prospect is already sold, keep talking and you will unsell them. Read the room. If they are ready to move, get to the close.

Never end the call without a next step. Vague endings kill deals. "I will follow up" is not a next step. A specific date, a specific action, a specific outcome — that is a next step.

🚫
Common Pitch Mistakes

Pitching before discovery is complete. You do not know enough yet to make it relevant. Finish the questions first.

Generic pitches. If your pitch sounds identical to the last five calls, you are not tailoring. Prospects feel this — it sounds like a script.

Feature dumping. Leading with a list of features before connecting to their pain. Lead with the outcome, then explain how you get there.

Skipping the value anchor. Going straight to price without confirming they see the value first. This creates sticker shock because there is no context for the number.

Talking through the silence. After presenting price, reps fill silence out of discomfort. The silence is part of the close. Do not rob it.

Ending without a committed next step. "I will send you some info" is a dead end. Every call ends with a specific, confirmed next step on the calendar.

🔒
Closing After the Pitch

"Based on everything we have covered — does this make sense for where you are trying to go?"

If yes: "Perfect. Let me get the agreement over to you today and we will get your slot reserved."

If maybe: "What would need to be true for this to be a yes?"

If no: "Appreciate the honesty — can I ask what the main thing is that is holding you back? I want to make sure I am not missing something."

The close is not a pressure move. It is the natural conclusion of a well-run conversation. If the discovery was thorough and the pitch was relevant, asking for the decision should feel like the obvious next step — not a confrontation.
