Standard SOP for CRM

The CRM is the single source of truth for every deal. This covers exactly what goes in, when it goes in, and what the standards are for every rep.

📌
The Standard

If it is not in the CRM, it does not exist. Every call, every touchpoint, every stage change is logged the same day it occurs — no exceptions.

The CRM is how the manager sees the pipeline. If your data is not current, leadership is managing blind — and so are you.

CRM compliance is not optional. Reps who consistently fail to update will be addressed directly — first in a 1on1, then formally if the behavior continues.

🗂️
Pipeline Stages
1

New Lead

Lead entered pipeline from outbound, referral, or inbound. Not yet contacted.

Owner: SDR — Action: First outreach within 24 hours

2

Contacted

Outreach has been sent. Awaiting a response.

Owner: SDR — Action: Follow up per sequence. Log every touchpoint same day.

3

Qualified

Lead responded and has been confirmed as a fit against ICP criteria.

Owner: SDR — Action: Book discovery call. Brief closer before the call.

4

Discovery Call Scheduled

Call is confirmed on the calendar.

Owner: Setter — Action: Confirm 24 hours before. Complete closer brief.

5

Discovery Call Completed

Call happened. Notes and recording logged.

Owner: Closer — Action: Send follow-up same day. Log call outcome and next step.

6

Proposal Sent

Scope and investment have been shared with the prospect.

Owner: Closer — Action: Follow up within 48 hours if no response.

7

Close Call Scheduled

Final decision call is on the calendar.

Owner: Closer — Action: Confirm attendance. Prepare objection responses.

8

Closed Won

Contract signed and payment confirmed.

Owner: Closer — Action: Notify manager. Trigger onboarding. Log deal value and close date.

9

Closed Lost

Prospect did not move forward. Loss reason required.

Owner: Closer — Action: Log specific loss reason. Add to Nurture if potential future fit.

10

Nurture

Not ready now. Revisit in 60-90 days.

Owner: SDR — Action: Set a follow-up task for 60 days out before moving on.

✅
Non-Negotiables
Every Contact Record Must Have

Company record associated and accurate

Contact title and LinkedIn URL

Lead source (outbound, referral, or inbound)

Last activity date and a next step task with a due date

Every Call Log Must Include

Date and outcome (moved forward, no-show, closed, lost)

Key notes — pain points, objections raised, what resonated

Confirmed next step with a specific date

Link to the call recording

Deal Rules

No deal sits in the same stage for more than 7 days without a logged activity

Deal value must be updated every time scope changes

Every Closed Lost deal must have a specific loss reason — no blanks

Do not mark a deal Won until the contract is signed and payment is confirmed

🔴
What Bad CRM Data Costs You

Management cannot see which deals need attention — good opportunities fall through the cracks.

You cannot calculate a real close rate — so you do not know if you are actually improving.

You cannot identify loss patterns — so you keep losing deals for the same fixable reason.

Automation sequences may not fire correctly if pipeline stages are not synced — prospects fall out of follow-up.

It signals to the team that process is optional. That standard does not exist here.
