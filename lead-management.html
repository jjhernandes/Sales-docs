Lead Management and Sales Process

The full process from the moment a lead enters the pipeline to a signed contract — who owns what, and what happens at every step.

🗺️
Full Process Overview
01

Lead Enters Pipeline

Outbound prospect or inbound inquiry. Logged in CRM immediately. Assigned to active outreach.

Owner: SDR

02

Outreach and First Contact

SDR runs the outbound sequence or responds to inbound within 5 minutes. Goal: earn a reply and confirm basic fit before moving forward.

Owner: SDR

03

Qualification

Confirm ICP fit: company profile, budget authority, and genuine interest. All three required before a closer's calendar is touched.

Owner: SDR / Setter

04

Discovery Call Booked

Call scheduled and confirmed. Pre-call automated sequence fires. Setter brief added to CRM contact record before the call.

Owner: Setter

05

Pre-Call Prep

Closer reads the setter brief. Reviews the prospect's company and any relevant context. Prepares 2-3 discovery angles based on what is known.

Owner: Closer

06

Discovery Call

Closer runs the call using the SIGNAL framework. Call recorded. Outcome and key notes logged in CRM same day. Follow-up email sent within 2 hours.

Owner: Closer

07

Post-Call Sequence

Automated follow-up sequence fires. Closer monitors for replies and responds to any engagement same day.

Owner: Automation + Closer on replies

08

Close Call Scheduled

Decision call booked. Closer confirms attendance and prepares objection responses. Pre-call sequence fires.

Owner: Closer

09

Close Call

Closer presents investment, anchors on value, uses entry offer if needed. Never ends the call without a confirmed next step.

Owner: Closer

10

Contract and Onboarding

Contract sent same day. Unsigned contracts followed up within 24 hours. Once signed: notify manager, trigger onboarding, log Closed Won in CRM.

Owner: Closer + Manager

🚦
Lead Qualification Standards
Must Have — All Three

Company matches your ideal client profile (ICP) in terms of size, industry, and stage

Contact owns or meaningfully influences the budget for the type of solution you sell

Company is actively investing in solving the problem your offer addresses — not just casually curious

Strong Fit Signals

Has tried other solutions and was disappointed — they know what bad looks like

Has a specific event or deadline creating urgency (product launch, funding round, leadership change)

Budget authority is clear — decision-maker is already in the conversation

Can articulate the problem in their own words — they have already thought about it

Do Not Book

Company does not fit your ICP — different industry, wrong size, wrong stage

No clear decision-maker involved or accessible

Looking for a one-time fix when your model requires ongoing commitment

Cannot invest at your price point — qualify this early, not on the close call

⏱️
Speed and Follow-Up Standards

Inbound inquiries: Responded to within 5 minutes during business hours.

New outbound leads: First outreach within 24 hours of entering the CRM.

Post-call follow-up: Sent within 2 hours of the call ending, every time.

Unsigned contracts: Followed up within 24 hours. Momentum dies fast after a call.

Deals with no activity: Any deal in the same stage for 7+ days gets flagged. Move it forward or close it out.

Nurture leads: 60-day follow-up task set before moving on. Do not let qualified prospects disappear.

🔁
No-Show and Ghosting Protocol
No-Show on a Booked Call

At 5 minutes: Send a quick message — "Hey [Name], I'm standing by. Still good to connect today?"

At 15 minutes: Send a reschedule link via email and a secondary channel.

24 hours later: One more outreach attempt with a direct ask to reschedule.

After 2 attempts with no response: Move to Nurture. Set a 60-day re-engage task. Do not chase further.

Going Dark After a Call

Let the automated post-call sequence run. It exists specifically for this scenario.

After the breakup message with no response: move to Nurture. Do not burn the relationship by over-chasing.

Log the outcome in the CRM with a specific reason — "No response after full follow-up sequence" is valid data. Use it.
