Section 1

Tom's Weekly Cadence

Monday
11:45 — Full team meeting. Entire sales team, all offices together. Recap of everything: what to expect, upcoming events, what to be planning for.
Mon / Wed / Fri
12:00 — Setting calls. Monday is projection-based: review last week's data, set expectations and a plan for the coming week, look back at past dates too. Wednesday is a training call, built around whatever feedback or topics setters bring up — an hour of training, some roleplay or practice. Friday is live call reviews, or another training/roleplay session, keeping everyone sharp on issues that came up that week.
Monday
30 min each with offer owners — Tom, Justin, and Tauha.
Rest of week
Fairly flexible. Walk, gym, and personal time before the 12:00 meetings; from 12:00 onward, reviewing data and working through everything.
Section 2

Tracking Systems Currently in Use

Four separate systems right now, none of them fully trusted.

1. Setter Tracking Sheet
Each setter (e.g. Liam) logs their own numbers day by day — first 20 days of the month shown as an example. Recently started adding notes and objections after Tom explained the gap; wasn't doing that before.
Percentage of leads looks unrealistically low, qualified set rate looks too high — likely not being filled in accurately across the team
2. Setaflow (Josiah's tool)
One DM setter (Josiah) brought this over from his own mentorship — running it alongside the main tracking. Goes through conversations per day plus a log of closes and sets. Looks like a nice format, but the underlying data needs to actually be kept current.
Show rate comes out higher than what's on the calendar — not fully accurate, and unclear how consistently Josiah is updating it
3. GoHighLevel Dashboard
Auto-tracks time on calls, outbound call volume, answered/missed/failed/busy breakdowns. Example day: 122 outbound calls, 106 marked "answered," 8 booked.
Answer numbers don't hold up — doesn't seem realistic that 106 of 122 dials actually got answered. Voicemails are getting counted as "answered" too, which drags average call time down to something like 10 seconds and skews the data further. The only genuinely reliable metric out of this dashboard is total time spent on calls.
4. Tom's Main Comparison Sheet
Used to compare all setters side by side and get a daily overview. Supplemented by manually checking GoHighLevel's Opportunities tab, filtered by each setter, to see how many leads they're actually moving through stages each day.
Section 3

The Core Problem

Four disconnected systems, one source of truth missing. The setter sheet, Setaflow, GoHighLevel, and Tom's comparison sheet all exist in parallel — none of them fully trusted on its own.
Manual entry isn't consistent. Even with reminders (which has helped recently), Tom can't be fully confident the team is filling things in accurately or every single day.
GoHighLevel's automated tracking has real gaps. Voicemails counting as answered calls, answer rates that don't pass a basic sanity check — the platform's own numbers can't be taken at face value.
Section 4

What Tom Is Actually Asking For

Option A: A better tracking system altogether — something similar to what's already being tried (like Setaflow), if there's a cleaner or more reliable version of that kind of setup.

Option B: Keep the existing tracking sheet, but get specific about exactly what needs to be filled in and how, so accuracy stops being a guessing game.

Either way, the underlying ask is the same: a way to actually trust the numbers — not four partial pictures that don't agree with each other.
