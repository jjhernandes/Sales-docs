Key Lessons from the Session
Respect First — Likability Later

John's biggest gift — energy, warmth, and social ease — is also his biggest risk if used too early. Don't try to win people over in the first 5 seconds. Come in slower. Build respect first. Let the warmth creep in gradually. By the time they feel it, they've already bought into you as an authority — not just a likable person.

JJ's line: "I slowly creep into it. I will slowly build rapport. And then by month 1, 2, I open up — and then I'll be like, what's up, man?"

Slow Down in Discovery — Don't Jump to Solutions

John noticed himself getting excited when hearing someone's problem and moving to solutions too fast — before fully understanding the depth of where they are. This robs the close before it starts. Stay in the problem longer. Let them articulate it fully. Their urgency is built by their own words, not your pitch.

This directly mirrors acting: stillness, presence, and surrender to the moment. He's already learned this — it's about applying it to sales.

Someone Has to Lead at All Times

JJ showed a live call recording (Matt, a rep in Florida) where the prospect took control of the conversation after 4 minutes of uninterrupted pitch. From that moment, it was never a sales call — it was a back-and-forth. If you don't lead, they do. And they will not close themselves.

Leadership on a call isn't aggressive — it's directional. Set the agenda, move the conversation, interrupt when necessary. The person who controls the frame controls the outcome.

Closing Is Not About What You Say

JJ's own honest admission: he's largely closed by providing so much value upfront that people come to him. When it's come time to hard-close, he's winged it. The program will fix this with a real framework — Week 4 covers exactly how to control the conversation, create the gap, and close deliberately.

The Map of Consciousness

JJ placed John in the love/reason/joy range — higher than most sales reps who operate from pride or fear. This is a meaningful thing: it affects how he handles loss, rejection, comparison, and pressure. JJ shared his own 15-minute guilt spiral in February and how he moved through it. How fast you recover from low states matters more than whether you hit them.

Book: Letting Go by Dr. David Hawkins. Audiobook works great.
