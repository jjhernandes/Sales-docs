Sales Operations

Frameworks and Scripts

The call framework and core scripts that structure every discovery and close call. Internalize these — do not read from them.

🧠
The Philosophy

Scripts are a starting point, not a crutch. The best reps use this framework as a mental map — not a word-for-word read.

Senior buyers can hear a script. What they respond to is a rep who listens, asks smart questions, and speaks directly. Internalizing the structure is what gets you there.

Master the framework, then make it yours. The structure does not change — the language should sound like you.

⚡
The SIGNAL Framework
S

Set the Agenda (2-3 min)

Take control of the call from the start. Remove pressure. Tell the prospect exactly how the call will run and get a verbal yes before continuing.

I

Investigate Current State (10-15 min)

Understand their situation fully before introducing your solution. Ask about what they are doing now, what is frustrating them, and what they have tried.

G

Get to the Gap (5-10 min)

Find out what is at stake if they do nothing. Attach a consequence or a number to the problem. Urgency is built here — not during the pitch.

N

Navigate the Solution (10-12 min)

Now introduce your offer — but tailor it specifically to what they just told you. Connect your solution to their stated pain point by point.

A

Anchor the Decision (5 min)

Before presenting price, get a verbal yes to the concept. Confirm they see the value before you introduce the investment number.

L

Lock In or Lower the Barrier (5-10 min)

Present the investment clearly. Pause. Let them respond. Use entry offers or phased options if they need to de-risk before committing fully.

Core Scripts
Opening — Set the Agenda

"Hey [Name], really appreciate you making time. Before we dive in, let me tell you how I like to run these. I'm going to ask you some questions to understand where you are and what you're trying to solve. If it looks like we can genuinely help, I'll walk you through exactly what that looks like. If it's not the right fit, I'll tell you that too — no wasted time on either side. Sound good?"

Wait for a yes. Do not proceed until they confirm.

Discovery — Core Questions

"Walk me through what you're currently doing to [solve the problem your offer addresses]."

"What has been the biggest frustration with how you've been handling this?"

"Have you tried other solutions or worked with other providers before? What was that experience like?"

"What would solving this actually mean for you and your business over the next 12 months?"

"Who else is involved when you make a decision like this?"

Gap Questions

"What happens if this problem is still there six months from now?"

"What does it cost you — in time, revenue, or opportunity — to not have this solved right now?"

"How urgent is this for you and your team right now?"

Anchoring Before Price

"Before I walk you through the investment, I want to make sure this actually makes sense for what you are trying to accomplish. Based on everything you shared — [reflect their goal back] — does this look like the kind of thing that would move the needle for you?"

Presenting the Investment

"Our [offer] runs from [low] to [high] depending on scope. Based on what you described, I would be looking at [range]. That includes [summarize key deliverables]."

Pause. Let them respond. Do not fill the silence.

🛡️
Objection Handling Framework

Objections are not rejections. They are requests for more information or more confidence. Never argue. Acknowledge, get curious about what is behind it, then address the real concern.

"We already have someone handling this."
"That makes sense. Can I ask — what specifically is working well with that, and is there anything it's not covering that brought you on this call today?"
"We don't have budget right now."
"I hear you on timing. Can I ask — what does it cost you to not solve this over the next 6 months? I'm not trying to pressure you, I just want to make sure we're looking at the full picture."
"How do we know this actually works?"
"Fair question — I'd rather show you than tell you. Let me walk you through a specific example of what this looked like for a client in a similar situation."
"I need to run this by [decision-maker]."
"Totally makes sense. Rather than leave it as an email to forward, would it make sense to get 30 minutes with [them] together? That usually moves faster and makes sure nothing gets lost in translation."
"The price is too high."
"I appreciate you being direct. Help me understand — is it that the budget isn't there, or is it that you're not yet confident enough in the ROI to justify it? Those are two different conversations."
