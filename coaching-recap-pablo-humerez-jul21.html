Context

Where Pablo is

A couple in their 60s — wife bought in, husband not. She'd separately messaged the setter asking about a competing lead magnet ("Finance to Freedom" challenge) nobody followed up on, then declined the $10K offer.

Live Deal Strategy

Re-engaging the stalled couple

→
What actually happened
Wife was bought in on the call itself — Pablo heard her say, under her breath, something like "it is just sitting there," while the husband kept his arms crossed and said only "we need further conversation." Separately, she'd messaged the setter asking what a competing lead magnet ("Finance to Freedom") was — nobody answered her — then sent a decline email citing minimal debt, being in their 60s, and not wanting to spend $10K on top of investing.
JJ's read: she doesn't know that Pablo is aware she asked about the other program, and it never got resolved. That's the opening — not to chase the decline, but to close the loop she's still waiting on.
→
The follow-up approach
Reference what she asked about directly, offer to close that loop, and use it as the reason to get back on a call — rather than letting the decline stand unanswered.
"I know you had asked about the Finance to Freedom program — if it's all right with you, we can jump back on and talk about what that looks like, make sure it's the best fit. And if it's not, I'll help you get started with what we discussed instead."
JJ Hernandez — suggested approach
JJ's broader point: don't let it die from silence. "Doing nothing is not better than doing something." If there's real conviction the $10K program is what she needs — and Pablo confirmed it is, given her stated goals — it's worth one more attempt before moving on.
→
Breaking the "fourth wall" for the husband
Both partners have to be bought in — one can't carry the other. The technique: name the asymmetry directly instead of pretending both were equally on board.
"Sarah, I see you're there. Todd, I see you're not. What's missing for you? What would you need to know for this to be a yes for you, just like it is for Sarah?"
JJ Hernandez
This turns it from "you two vs. the decision" into Pablo and Sarah gently bringing Todd on board together — rather than Pablo arguing against both of them at once.
Coaching Moments

What was worked through

01
Know the lead source before the call
Pablo didn't know whether a given lead came from a paid ad, organic content, or a lead magnet — and there's no SOP documenting it. JJ's point: this materially changes how you approach the call. A retargeting ad, a direct-book ad, and an organic lead-magnet opt-in are three different conversations with three different levels of buying intent already built in.
"You're selling the person, not the product. If we're thinking about the customer journey, we need to know where they came from."
JJ Hernandez
02
Pick one framework and commit — stop blending two
Pablo had been mixing two approaches: a "goals first" open (from a Loom Tristan's team lead recorded) and a "biggest challenge first" open (from Tristan's actual script), both feeding into a Situation → Problem → Solution → Goal/Future Pace/Consequence structure. Both openers technically work — the issue was that prospects would respond to either one with a flood of current-state, future-state, and emotional content all mixed together, and Pablo didn't have a rule for what to grab first.
JJ's fix: stay strictly in current reality until it's fully mapped. Anything the prospect volunteers about the future gets set aside — acknowledged, not chased — until the situation and problem stages are actually complete. Mixing the order of the frameworks (going future → current reality) is possible, but it means flipping the whole structure on purpose, not doing it by accident mid-call.
"Once you get comfortable, you can free-flow. That's the benefit. Right now you're just not comfortable with any of them — so stick to one."
JJ Hernandez
03
You control the call — don't make yourself smaller to let them run
Pablo's instinct was to chase every interesting thread a prospect gave him, worried he'd "miss" something valuable. JJ's reframe: that's the prospect controlling the call, not Pablo. The fix isn't to remember everything — it's to interrupt respectfully and redirect: acknowledge what was said, then pull the conversation back to the current stage.
"You gave me a lot of information there — I appreciate the context, but I want to understand where you're at right now. What do you believe is your biggest challenge, specifically?"
JJ Hernandez — example redirect
The underlying principle: every decision is rooted in clarity — for the rep first. If you don't understand the situation clearly enough to explain it back, you can't actually help them, no matter how much they've said.
Live Practice

Roleplay — catching it in real time

▶
JJ played an erratic prospect; Pablo ran the open
JJ fed Pablo a rambling answer mixing income level, lifestyle spending, and an identity-level statement ("I guess I'm just ignorant about it") — deliberately similar to what real prospects had been doing to him. Pablo's first instinct was to chase the identity statement ("ignorant") and go deep on it immediately, which stalled him out.
Pablo
"Now to get more specific, I kind of want to understand what's not working to where you're not able to spend your money better."
"Prospect"
"I guess it's an education thing... an ignorance thing. I don't come from money. No one ever taught me financial literacy."
Pablo
(pauses, unsure where to go — starts trying to unpack "ignorant" as an identity issue)
JJ (as coach)
"That would have been a great question to ask: 'What do you mean by that?' You're getting too advanced — stick to the process. You just need to understand the situation."
The correction: when a prospect drops something loaded ("ignorant," "erratic spending"), the next move is the simple clarifying question — "What do you mean by that? How so?" — not an immediate deep dive into what it means about who they are. Identity-level work comes later, once the situation is fully understood.
Pablo's Observations

What clicked by the end

→
Naming his own pattern: Pablo recognized mid-session that his instinct to "dig deep" on everything was actually him losing control, not being thorough — unprompted, before JJ fully spelled it out.
→
The redirect line works both ways: Pablo saw that stopping to ask "what do you mean by that" doesn't just clarify — it slows the prospect down and builds productive tension, rather than being a compromise between control and curiosity.
→
End-of-session confidence: Pablo confirmed the framework distinction landed — closed the session saying he could now slow prospects down and knew what to grab first when they gave him a wall of information.
