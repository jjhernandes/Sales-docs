Setter call process for the Alex Eubank personal brand offer. Built from Fernando's own framework plus coaching from JJ — April 17, 2026.

🧠
The Mindset Going In

You are qualifying them — not selling to them. The frame is: are they a good fit to work with Alex? That posture alone does most of the work. Let it.

Associated authority is already working for you. The brand does the heavy lifting. Prospects assume competence before you say a word. Don't fight it — anchor into it.

Stay detached from the outcome. You're not trying to close them. You're trying to understand if they belong here. That detachment is what creates the pull.

Clarity is the job. Most prospects don't fully know what they want or why. Your questions help them see it. When they get clear, they self-qualify — you don't have to push.

🗺️
The Call Framework
01

Intro — Set the Frame

Take control from the first sentence. Tell them exactly what this call is. Get a verbal yes before moving on.

"Hey, it's Fernando from Alex Eubank's team. The way we run these — I'm going to ask you some questions to understand where you're at with your personal brand, what you're looking to accomplish, and whether it makes sense for us to work together. Does that work for you?"

02

Goal State — Where Are They Trying to Go

Start with the future, not the present. Get clarity on what they actually want — not just a surface answer. Push until they're specific.

What are you trying to accomplish with your personal brand?
What does the personal brand actually look like for you — what is it about?
Why do you want to accomplish this? What's the real reason?
Why is it important to you specifically?
How long have you been wanting to do this?
Coaching Note

When they give a vague answer — "when you say grow it the right way, what does that really mean to you?" — press one level deeper. This is where the real reason comes out. Don't move on until it's specific.

03

Current Situation — Where Are They Now

Understand the full picture of their life right now. What they're doing, whether they enjoy it, and what they've already tried. This is where you start building the gap — not by asking about it directly, but by letting them describe it themselves.

What are you doing right now — are you working, or is the personal brand your main focus?
Do you enjoy what you're doing? Do you like the job?
What have you already done to try to grow the personal brand?
What's worked? What hasn't?
How long have you been trying to make this happen?
Why do you think you haven't been able to get it where you want it yet?
Coaching Note

"How long have you been trying? Why do you think you haven't been able to?" — this is the door. Doubt opens here. They weren't thinking about it before the question. Now they are. Don't rush past this moment. Sit in it.

04

Why Now — Create Urgency Through Awareness

Don't force a consequence question. Stay in discovery and let them find the gap themselves. The goal is for them to recognize their own discrepancy — not to be told it.

Why do you want to make this move now specifically?
What happens if you keep doing what you're doing and nothing changes?
For someone your age — most people aren't even thinking about this. Why are you?
Coaching Note

If they say "not much" or "I don't really know" when asked about their biggest struggle — don't accept it. Push back naturally: "There has to be something — why else would you be on this call?" Then let them think. They know. They just need a second to access it. Give them that second.

05

Commitment Check — Time and Investment

Before setting the call, confirm they're actually serious. Not a soft maybe — a real commitment to solving the problem if the right solution exists.

If we can show you a clear path to get this done the right way — is this something you're ready to move on?
How committed are you to making this happen this year?
Is investment something you're open to, assuming it makes sense?
06

Set the Call

Book the closer's call only if all three are confirmed: they fit the ICP, they have a real reason why, and they're open to investing. Don't book anyone who can't check all three.

"Based on everything you shared, I think it makes sense for you to get on a call with the team and see exactly what this looks like for your situation. Let me get you on the calendar — I've got [time] and [time] available. What works best for you?"

🔑
The Key Principles
Discovery Over Consequence

Don't hunt for a place to drop the consequence question. Stay deeper in discovery, and they will find the gap themselves. The goal is for them to recognize their own discrepancies — not to be pushed toward them. Questions that create self-awareness are more powerful than questions that create pressure.

Future to Current

Starting with the goal state (future) before the current situation is the right order for this offer. It anchors the conversation in what they want, so that when you get to the current situation, the gap is already visible to them — you don't have to manufacture it.

When They Say "I Don't Know"

They do know. They just haven't said it yet. Don't let them off the hook — just make it easy: "You know what it is. Just think about it." Then stay quiet. Let the silence work. They'll get there.

Use the Brand — Don't Ignore It

The association with Alex Eubank creates assumed authority before you open your mouth. Lean into it. Your posture should reflect it: you're not selling, you're evaluating. That frame — "are you good enough to work with us?" — is more powerful than any sales script on this offer.
